A Passion for Baking, Shared with Joy

Here, you’ll find a collection of my favorite cakes in the gallery along with base recipes, baking tips, and little tricks I’ve picked up along the way. Whether you’re just starting out or looking for inspiration, I hope this space brings you joy and confidence in the art of baking.

I’m not a professional baker and I don’t take orders but I bake with love for the people in my life, often for special occasions. For me, baking is a way to express love, share happiness, and celebrate moments with others. Every cake I make is crafted with care, passion, and a little piece of my heart.

Join me on this sweet journey as we explore the world of baking together!

Happy baking!

My Favorite cakes

Each cake in this gallery holds a special place in my heart. They’re more than just bakes they’re stories, memories, and little celebrations of love. I’ve never had formal training, and I’m still learning with every cake I make. The shapes may not be perfect, but the joy, creativity, and care I pour into each one are very real.

I baked my first cake at 9 years old using a pressure cooker on a gas stove. Life got busy and baking took a back seat, but after moving to Finland where ovens are part of every home I reconnected with that childhood passion.

My early attempts weren’t perfect: one cake was underbaked, and I once frosted a warm cake that completely melted! But I never gave up. I kept going, eventually baking for friends’ birthdays, anniversaries, and gatherings. Along the way, I’ve made both playful, decorated cakes and simple favorites like carrot cake, Finnish mocha brownies (mokkapala), and Brita cake my all time favorite!

I hope you enjoy browsing these sweet moments as much as I loved creating them.

Baking Tips

Here, I’m sharing some of my personal baking tips for those who are fascinated by baking but still feel a bit nervous to try.

  • I found a great recipe for a cake base on valio.fi that works really well for me. I love that it doesn’t use butter and instead is packed with protein it uses lots of eggs and also potato starch. I highly recommend it!
  • Don’t open the oven door too many times to check if the cake is baked. If you’re unsure, try to check without removing the cake from the oven. Changing the oven temperature while baking can affect the result.
  • Avoid using butter for frosting if your cake will be stored in the fridge. Cold temperatures can make butter-based frostings hard.
  • Chill your cake in the fridge before frosting. This helps the frosting hold better and stay in place.
  • If you're afraid of making sugar syrup for layering, try using fruit juice instead. It works beautifully and adds extra flavor to your cake.
  • When I have leftover cake, I freeze it and later turn it into cake pops they’re delicious, melt in your mouth, and taste amazing!
  • Don’t be afraid to use your creativity! Believe me, baking with love always gives the best results.
