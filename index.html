Hi, I'm Srilakshmi

So glad to see you here!

I'm a curious soul and a full-time working mom to two wonderful kids, an 11-year-old and a 4-year-old. Life has taken me across cultures, and one of the most defining chapters began when I moved from India to Finland.

Here, I discovered a new passion: baking! Though not a professional, I love making cakes for friends’ birthdays and small gatherings. It brings me joy, and I’m excited to share some of my creations, tips, and personal baking moments with you.

This blog is also where I share my life in Finland from navigating the education system and long winters, to enjoying traditions like the sauna. It’s been a journey full of learning, and I hope my stories help or inspire anyone curious about life here.

A cherished family moment captured on a beautiful autumm day.

I’m an Indian mom who grew up in a small village, surrounded by green fields, cousins, festivals, and endless cups of chai. Life was cozy, familiar, and full of laughter. I fell in love with my husband during my teenage years (yes, like a movie plot but with fewer songs and more real-life drama!). I’ve always liked things to be simple and predictable no big surprises, just the usual dosa for breakfast and the same lovely people around me.

Back home, I worked as a cashier in a rural bank, and my husband was in IT (of course, what else?). Life was cruising along smooth and sweet. We had our first baby girl and were enjoying our family life.

Then, boom! Life threw us a snowball. My husband got an opportunity to work in Finland. After lots of chai-fueled discussions and some emotional 'should we or shouldn’t we' moments, I left my job, we packed our bags (and spices), and moved to this land of snow and silence.
